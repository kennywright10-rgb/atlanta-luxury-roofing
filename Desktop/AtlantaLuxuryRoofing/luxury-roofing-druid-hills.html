Preservation-Grade Roofing for Druid Hills' Historic Homes

Druid Hills is unlike any other Atlanta neighborhood. Designed by Frederick Law Olmsted and developed primarily between 1910 and 1935, it is a National Register historic district where roofing decisions carry architectural and preservation weight that goes beyond any other residential market in the city.

Natural slate is the original and still-correct material for most Druid Hills homes -- Welsh, Vermont, or Spanish slate matched to the original specification wherever possible. We have sourced replacement slate for active Druid Hills roofs, navigated Druid Hills Civic Association review, and worked alongside preservation architects on full restorations of neighborhood landmark properties.

For Druid Hills homeowners whose original slate has reached the end of its life, we provide honest assessments, period-accurate material sourcing, and the kind of detailed workmanship these homes were built to receive.

Common Questions

Druid Hills Roofing FAQ

What roofing materials are appropriate for Druid Hills historic homes?

+

Natural slate is the period-correct and most commonly required material for Druid Hills homes, particularly those built between 1910 and 1935. Welsh, Vermont, and Spanish slate are the primary options. Copper flashing, gutters, and valleys are the preservation-correct detail materials. The Druid Hills Civic Association typically requires materials that match or authentically replicate the original construction.

Does the Druid Hills Civic Association approve roofing projects?

+

Yes. The Druid Hills Civic Association requires review and approval for exterior changes including roofing material replacement. We prepare all required documentation, including material specifications, historic precedent references, and color matching data, as part of our standard service for Druid Hills projects.

How much does slate roofing cost in Druid Hills?

+

Natural slate roofing in Druid Hills typically ranges from $35,000 for smaller cottage-scale homes to $200,000+ for large estate properties with complex rooflines. The sourcing of period-matching slate for partial replacements and the complexity of original Druid Hills roofline geometries place most projects in the $80,000–$180,000 range.

Can I replace my Druid Hills slate roof with a synthetic material?

+

The Druid Hills Civic Association generally requires authentic natural slate for properties within the historic district, not synthetic alternatives. We work exclusively with natural slate for Druid Hills restoration projects and can document the material specification for association review and approval.

Who handles permits for Druid Hills roofing projects?

+

Druid Hills is an unincorporated DeKalb County community, so roofing permits are issued through DeKalb County. We manage all permit applications, inspections, and sign-offs, as well as coordinating Druid Hills Civic Association review in parallel to keep the project moving on schedule.
