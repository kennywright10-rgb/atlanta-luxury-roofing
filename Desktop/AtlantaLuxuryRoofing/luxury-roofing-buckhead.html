We Serve All of Buckhead

Every Buckhead Community

Buckhead is not one neighborhood — it's a collection of distinct residential communities, each with its own character, architectural style, and roofing needs. We know all of them.

Tuxedo Park

Buckhead's most prestigious enclave. Large estate lots, mature tree canopy, predominantly traditional architecture. Vermont slate and copper are the norm on these properties.

Haynes Manor

Established luxury neighborhood with a mix of traditional and transitional homes. Standing seam metal increasingly popular for major renovations alongside existing slate repairs.

West Paces Ferry Corridor

Iconic Buckhead address. Large properties, many historically significant. Governors and ambassadors have lived here. Materials need to match the pedigree of the street.

Peachtree Heights

Elegant pre-war and post-war homes, many with original slate in need of restoration or replacement. We specialize in matching replacement slates to existing roofs.

Chastain Park

Surrounding one of Atlanta's premier parks. A mix of renovated ranches and new construction luxury. Standing seam metal dominates new builds in this area.

Garden Hills / Peachtree Hills

Charming smaller-scale luxury homes with strong character architecture. Cedar shake and slate both appear here. Careful material matching is critical on these distinctive homes.

Buckhead Roofing FAQ

Luxury roofing projects in Buckhead typically range from $55,000 to $400,000+ depending on home size, material, and complexity. Standing seam metal for Buckhead homes averages $85,000–$185,000. Natural slate projects run $120,000–$350,000 for typical estate sizes (4,000–8,000 sq ft). Copper roofing ranges from $180,000–$400,000+. Projects on West Paces Ferry and in Tuxedo Park tend toward the higher end due to home scale and architectural complexity.
For traditional Buckhead architecture — Georgian, Tudor, Colonial, French Provincial — natural slate is historically the most appropriate material, particularly Vermont or Buckingham slate. Buckhead's most prestigious addresses were built with slate as a matter of architectural integrity. For transitional and contemporary homes, standing seam metal (often in Charcoal or Patina Green) is increasingly the premium choice. We assess your specific architecture and advise accordingly during the inspection.

Natural slate projects range from 3–8 weeks depending on roof complexity, pitch, and the number of architectural details (dormers, turrets, valleys). Material lead times are discussed during the proposal phase — some premium Vermont slate and copper materials have 4–8 week lead times that affect overall scheduling.
