Common Questions

What our clients ask before we meet

For a typical Atlanta home, a professionally installed standing seam metal roof runs between $18,000 and $75,000 depending on square footage, roof complexity, metal type (steel, aluminum, or zinc), and whether any structural work is required. Homes in the 3,500–5,000 sq ft range with moderate complexity typically land between $30,000 and $55,000. We provide exact numbers in a written proposal after our on-site assessment — no ranges, no surprises.

Our projects typically run 4–10 weeks from contract signing to final walkthrough. Standing seam installs on a straightforward roofline run 2–4 weeks. Natural slate projects — which require careful layout, hand-selection of material, and precision flashing work — often run 6–10 weeks on a larger home. We commit to a timeline in our written proposal and communicate proactively if anything changes.

We manage the permit process in full and have worked with HOA architectural committees across Buckhead, Milton, Sandy Springs, and historic preservation boards in Druid Hills and Virginia-Highland. We know what documentation each requires and prepare it as part of our standard process. You don't manage the paperwork — we do.

Standing seam has concealed fasteners — panels interlock via raised seams with no screw heads exposed to weather. This eliminates the most common failure point of metal roofs (water infiltration around aging fastener holes), produces the clean architectural look found on high-end homes, and extends the roof's serviceable life significantly. Exposed fastener metal panels are cheaper but require more maintenance and are rarely appropriate for the homes we work on.

Significantly. Georgia's climate — hot humid summers, periodic ice events, and storm activity — is one of the things we discuss in every consultation. Steel and aluminum standing seam perform exceptionally here. Natural slate is essentially climate-indifferent and is an excellent long-term choice for Atlanta. Cedar shake requires more maintenance in humid climates and is typically recommended when architectural authenticity or preservation is the priority. We'll give you our honest recommendation for your specific home.
