Pricing Factors

What Drives Your Roofing Cost

■

Roof Area & Pitch

Steeper roofs require more material and more time. A 12/12 pitch takes significantly longer to install safely than an 8/12. Most Atlanta luxury homes have 8/12 to 12/12 pitches, which is already priced into our estimates.

■

Complexity & Details

Every valley, dormer, skylight, and chimney adds labor time. Copper flashing at each penetration is mandatory on premium jobs — it lasts as long as the roof itself and prevents leaks where lesser materials would fail.

■

Material Choice

Standing seam steel starts around $16/sqft installed. Natural slate runs $35–$65/sqft depending on origin. Copper tops out at $120+/sqft for full-roof applications. The material is typically 50–65% of total project cost.

■

Tear-Off & Decking

Removing an existing roof adds $2–$4 per sqft. If the decking underneath is damaged (common in older Atlanta homes), replacement adds another $3–$6/sqft. We always inspect decking before finalizing bids.

■

Permits & HOA

Fulton County permits run $400–$1,200 for roofing projects. Historic districts (Druid Hills, Inman Park) require additional review. Cherokee County permits for Milton projects typically add 1–2 weeks to timelines.

■

Seasonality & Lead Times

Natural slate and copper have 6–14 week material lead times from specialty suppliers. Summer projects carry slight labor premiums due to demand. Scheduling 90–120 days out typically secures the best pricing and crew availability.
