Why Copper

A Material That Only Improves With Age

Copper has roofed the world’s great buildings for over 2,000 years — from European cathedrals to American courthouses — because no other material matches its combination of longevity, beauty, and zero-maintenance performance.

A copper roof installed today will still be in service when your grandchildren inherit the home.

The Living Roof

How Copper Patina Develops Over Time

Unlike any other roofing material, copper is a living finish that transforms beautifully over decades. Atlanta’s humidity and rainfall accelerate the patination process, often producing rich tones faster than drier climates.

Year 1–3

Bright Copper

Fresh installation shows the warm, bright penny-copper tone. Develops a light surface oxidation that gives depth.

Year 3–8

Bronze & Sienna

Deepens to rich bronze and sienna tones. The surface feels more settled and the material gains a subtle texture.

Year 8–15

Dark Brown

Transitions to a deep chocolate brown. Often considered the most classically elegant phase of copper’s life.

Year 15–25

Blue-Green Verdigris

The signature patina arrives — blue-green verdigris that is both beautiful and protective. This phase lasts indefinitely.

Common Questions

Copper Roofing FAQ

How much does copper roofing cost in Atlanta?

+

Copper roofing in Atlanta ranges from $45 to $120 per square foot installed. Accent elements like cupolas or dormer caps start at $8,000–$25,000. Full copper standing seam roofs on a 4,000 sq ft home typically run $180,000–$380,000. Copper gutters and downspouts are $35–$65 per linear foot.

How long does copper roofing last in Atlanta’s climate?

+

Copper roofing routinely lasts 100+ years. Georgia’s heat and humidity accelerate the beautiful patina process, transitioning from bright copper to brown to the signature blue-green verdigris over 15–25 years. Copper requires virtually no maintenance and becomes more distinctive with age.

What copper applications are most popular in Buckhead?

+

In Buckhead and Sandy Springs, the most popular copper applications are standing seam accent sections over dormers and bay windows, copper gutters and half-round downspouts, cupola caps, and decorative flashing. Full copper roofs are occasionally specified on estate properties in Tuxedo Park and West Paces Ferry.

Does copper roofing increase home value?

+

Yes. Copper consistently adds to perceived and appraised value on Atlanta luxury homes. Appraisers in Fulton and Cherokee counties recognize copper as a premium material, and real estate agents in Buckhead and Milton report copper details differentiate listings and support higher asking prices.

Can copper be combined with slate or cedar shake?

+

Yes. A common Atlanta approach is natural slate on the main field with copper flashing, gutters, valleys, and cupola caps. This delivers the longevity of slate with the architectural drama of copper accents at a fraction of a full copper roof cost.
