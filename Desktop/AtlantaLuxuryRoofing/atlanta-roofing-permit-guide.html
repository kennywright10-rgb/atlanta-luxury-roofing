Atlanta Roofing Permits: What You Need to Know

Updated April 2026  •  7-minute read  •  Atlanta, Georgia

Every significant roofing project in the Atlanta metro area requires a permit. The specific requirements, fees, timelines, and inspection processes vary by jurisdiction — sometimes dramatically. Here's what Atlanta luxury homeowners need to know before the project starts.

Why Permits Matter on Luxury Roofs

Unpermitted roofing work creates real liability for Atlanta homeowners. Insurance companies can deny claims on unpermitted roofs. Buyers' inspectors identify permit records gaps during sale transactions. HOAs increasingly require permit numbers as part of improvement approval processes.

For luxury roofing materials — slate, copper, standing seam metal — the installation complexity and project value make proper permitting even more important. These materials require specific installation methods that inspectors are trained to verify.

Jurisdiction by Neighborhood

City of Atlanta (Buckhead, Virginia-Highland, Morningside, Inman Park): Atlanta's Bureau of Buildings issues roofing permits. Online applications through the city's portal. Standard residential roofing permits process in 5–10 business days. Historic district projects (Inman Park, Druid Hills) require additional Atlanta Urban Design Commission review, adding 2–4 weeks.

Fulton County (unincorporated, Alpharetta, Sandy Springs, Johns Creek): Fulton County permit portal. Sandy Springs and Alpharetta operate their own building departments as independent cities. Standard processing: 7–14 business days.

Cobb County (East Cobb, Vinings, unincorporated): Cobb County Department of Community Development. Online permitting available. Standard processing: 5–10 business days. City of Marietta has its own department.

Cherokee County (Milton, portions of Alpharetta): Cherokee County Community Development. Milton operates its own permits department. Slightly longer processing common: 10–15 business days. Milton has additional design review requirements for certain exterior changes.

DeKalb County (Druid Hills, Brookhaven, Dunwoody): DeKalb County Permit Office. City of Brookhaven and City of Dunwoody each have independent permit departments. Standard processing: 7–14 business days.

Typical Permit Fees

Roofing permit fees in Atlanta metro jurisdictions are typically calculated as a percentage of project value or a flat fee schedule based on project scope. For a $60,000 luxury roofing project, expect permit fees of $400–$1,200 depending on jurisdiction. Some jurisdictions also charge inspection fees separately.

HOA Approvals

Most Atlanta luxury communities require HOA architectural review committee approval before exterior changes. This is separate from the municipal permit process and runs in parallel. Approval requirements vary by HOA, but typically require material specifications, color samples, and contractor licensing documentation.

Timelines vary widely: some HOA committees meet monthly; others have delegated approval authority to individual review officers who can approve within days. We manage all HOA submissions as part of our standard project process.

Historic District Requirements

The most complex permit scenarios involve historic districts. Druid Hills (Dekalb County historic overlay), Inman Park (National Register, City of Atlanta review), and portions of Morningside and Virginia-Highland all have additional design review requirements.

These reviews evaluate material authenticity, color appropriateness, and profile compatibility with the district's historic character. Copper, natural slate, and period-appropriate cedar shake typically receive approval. Modern synthetic materials are reviewed case-by-case.
